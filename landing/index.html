01

PGP in the browser, before it moves

OpenPGP.js encrypts every hollr client-side with your public key before a single byte leaves the sender's device. What travels through our servers is ciphertext — unreadable without your private key.

02

We generate your keys — or you come correct with your own

hollr creates a fresh PGP keypair on setup and hands you the private key. Or paste your existing public key. Either way, we never see the private side — ever.

03

Your inbox, your rules

By default, the PGP-armored payload routes through yo@hollr.to. Plug in your own Resend key and it goes direct — not through a shared pool, not a hollr relay. Either way, same encryption, zero storage.

04

Decrypt on your own turf — no PGP client needed

No GPG installed? No problem. Open the hollr decryptor, paste your private key and the ciphertext, and it decrypts locally in your browser. Nothing leaves the tab. No install. No excuses.

Roll with your own keys

Already rocking PGP? Paste your public key on setup and every incoming hollr gets encrypted with it from the start. Your private key never goes near our servers — not even during onboarding. You stay in control.

hollr-decrypt.js 
// Decrypt in your browser — nothing leaves this tab
const { data } = await openpgp.decrypt({
  message: await openpgp.readMessage({ armoredMessage }),
  decryptionKeys: privateKey,
});
return data; // your message, decrypted locally
